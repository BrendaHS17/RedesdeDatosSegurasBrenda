6G

As its name suggests, is the sixth generation of mobile connectivity. Just as 4G succeeded 3G and 5G succeeded 4G , 6G will come to replace 5G with higher speed and even lower latency.

In addition to greater bandwidth and speeds much higher than 5G , 6G will also offer many other advantages. One of them will be the reduction of latency, which will be 0.1 milliseconds. This becomes a near real-time data stream.

On the other hand, there is also talk of an even greater reduction in energy consumption. We remember that 5G is very energy efficient, reducing 4G consumption by 90%. Although there are no exact data yet, 6G will once again reduce electricity consumption considerably. Another point to note is that 6G will increase the number of simultaneous device connections. All this will enable the use of new and still unexplored technologies

Wifi 6

Wi-Fi 6 is a substantial improvement over previous generations, though the differences may not seem immediately obvious to the average user. These changes may not drastically alter the way we use wireless routers or networks, but consist of many enhancements that, when combined, result in a substantial upgrade. The first big change is that Wi-Fi 6 allows for potentially faster connection speeds.

With Wi-Fi 6, organizations and service providers can support new and emerging applications on the same wireless LAN (WLAN) infrastructure while simultaneously providing a higher level of service to legacy applications. This landscape lays the foundation for new business models and increased adoption of Wi-Fi.

Faster Wi-Fi means faster upload and download speeds (or data throughput) due to the increased bandwidth made possible by Wi-Fi 6. This becomes more and more important as file sizes increase, coupled with increased data demands for high-quality video streaming and communication-heavy online gaming.
